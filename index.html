BMES Heart Phantom Design Team

Filling the need for multimodal, dynamic, myocardial imaging phantoms

PROBLEM STATEMENT

There is a need for a dynamic imaging phantom for acquisition of myocardial images in order to test these new imaging strategies. Currently, available phantoms are unimodal and very expensive. Thus, we present a cost-effective, dynamic, multimodal phantom for the accurate calibration and testing of novel imaging sequences.

TIMELINE

November 2015 to December 2015

Brainstormed ideas for overall design and structure. Researched similar projects to find ways to make our design an improvement on previous heart phantoms. Began testing materials to make our heart replica, including PVA, PDMS, PEG, agar, and natural rubber latex.

Read more Jan 14

January 2016

Started prototyping and 3D-printing two-part molds of the left ventricle of a rabbit heart. Chose a low-cost, biocompatible, silicone-based material to make the heart replica. Began building pump system.

Read more Jan 18

February 2016

Refined overall design and focused on assembly. Initially dealt with uneven density in the walls of the heart phantom, but continued to refine/test various 3D printed molds. Ultimately developed a novel design for the 3D printed molds which allows us to incorporate physical deformities in the heart phantom.

Read more Jan 24

March 2016

Wrote Arduino code for stepper motor driving our pump system using discrete data from a PPG graph. Presented prototype and poster at Engineering Open House at UIUC.

Read more Feb 14

April 2016

Further refined prototype by upgrading the stepper motor, reducing heart chamber size, and creating a cylindrical casing for the heart. Explored more physiologically accurate heart models using the CAD files in the National Institute of Health's (NIH) Heart Library to create negatives for a full heart mold.

Read more Feb 18

May 2016 to June 2016

Wrote and submitted reports to various competitions in order to recieve funding to continue our device development.
